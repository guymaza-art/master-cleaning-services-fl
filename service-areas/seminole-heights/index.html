FAQ — HVAC Cleaning in Seminole Heights, FL

I just moved into a renovated Seminole Heights bungalow — should I clean the ducts first?

Yes, before you settle in. Renovation projects disturb enormous amounts of construction dust, old insulation, and potentially lead paint particles in pre-1978 homes. Even if the previous owner did a beautiful renovation, they almost certainly did not clean the ductwork. A pre-move-in cleaning starts your time in the home with clean air.

My Craftsman home has original wood floors — will duct cleaning make dust worse temporarily?

No. We use negative-pressure systems that contain debris inside the duct system and our equipment rather than releasing it into your home. In fact, after cleaning, you'll typically notice less dust settling on your hardwood floors because the HVAC system is no longer recirculating it.

Do the fireplaces in Seminole Heights bungalows affect duct systems?

Yes. Original Craftsman bungalows in Seminole Heights typically have decorative or functional fireplaces. If the chimney isn't properly sealed, downdrafts can push soot and ash into adjacent wall cavities and duct systems. We recommend annual chimney sweeping ($99) in addition to duct cleaning for any home with a working fireplace.

How often should Seminole Heights bungalows get duct cleaning?

Every 2–3 years for standard occupancy. If you've recently completed a renovation, immediately after. If you have pets or allergy sufferers in the home, annually. The 100-year-old housing stock in Seminole Heights accumulates contaminants faster than modern construction due to rougher duct surfaces and less-sealed construction.
