  • Original 1960s–1980s duct systems in Largo homes frequently have never been professionally cleaned — decades of lint, pet dander, and biological growth are typical finds
  • Rental property cycling means Largo ducts see high occupant turnover without maintenance — new tenants inherit years of prior-occupant buildup
  • Many Largo homes use older galvanized sheet metal ductwork with mastic tape seams that have dried and separated — we flag any gaps that let conditioned air escape

FAQ — HVAC Cleaning in Largo, FL

How do you handle older duct systems in Largo's 1960s and 1970s homes?

Older duct systems in Largo homes are often fiberglass duct board or galvanized sheet metal — both common from the 1960s through 1980s. We adjust our process accordingly: low-pressure agitation on duct board to avoid liner damage, and wire brushing on metal ducts that have decades of buildup. We inspect the system before touching anything.

Do you offer services for Largo rental property owners?

Yes — we work with many Largo landlords who need cleaning between tenants or on an annual maintenance schedule. We can provide before-and-after photos documenting the work, which is useful for property records. Ask about volume pricing for multiple rental units.

How often do Largo homes need duct cleaning?

Homes with original 1960s–1980s ductwork in Largo should be cleaned every 2–3 years at minimum. Heavy rental use, multiple tenants, and pets can push that to every 12–18 months. If duct cleaning has never been done, expect us to find significant buildup.

What does dryer vent cleaning cost in Largo?

Dryer vent cleaning in Largo starts at $79. Older Largo homes and apartments often have short wall-exit vent paths, but blockage from years of lint accumulation and crushed plastic flexible duct is very common. We replace damaged flex sections if needed.

Do you service apartment buildings and multi-family in Largo?

We handle residential units in multi-family buildings — we clean each individual unit's HVAC system. For buildings with shared mechanical spaces, we advise contacting the HOA or property manager to discuss common-area equipment. Call us to discuss your specific property.
