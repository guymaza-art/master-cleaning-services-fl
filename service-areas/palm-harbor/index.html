FAQ — HVAC Cleaning in Palm Harbor, FL

Does salt air from the Gulf affect my AC coils in Palm Harbor?

Yes — homes within a few miles of the Gulf accumulate salt-air corrosion on aluminum evaporator fins. This restricts airflow and, when combined with humidity, accelerates biological growth on the coil. Palm Harbor homes near the water need coil cleaning every 12–18 months rather than the standard 2-year cadence.

Do you service condos near Innisbrook and the Palm Harbor waterfront?

Yes — we work in condos and HOA communities throughout Palm Harbor, including Innisbrook-area units, coastal mid-rises, and interior subdivisions. Condo units often share ventilation chases; we clean each unit's individual system.

My Palm Harbor condo has older ductwork — is it safe to clean?

We inspect the ductwork during our free walk-through before any cleaning. 1970s–1980s fiberglass duct board is common in older Palm Harbor condos. We use low-pressure brushing and suction on older systems to avoid damaging fragile duct lining.
