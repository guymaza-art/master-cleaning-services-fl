FAQ — HVAC Cleaning in Carrollwood, FL

My Carrollwood home was built in 1978 — should the original ducts be replaced or just cleaned?

In most cases, cleaning is sufficient. Well-maintained original ductwork from the late 1970s is often still structurally sound. We assess each system and provide an honest recommendation. If we find deteriorating flex duct liner or significant corrosion, we'll document it and advise on next steps. The cleaning alone typically extends system life and improves air quality dramatically.

Oak pollen season in Tampa makes my allergies terrible — will duct cleaning help?

Yes, significantly. Spring oak pollen that accumulates inside ductwork recirculates for months after pollen season ends. Cleaning your ducts after spring pollen season removes this stored allergen source, providing relief long after outdoor pollen counts have dropped. Many Carrollwood allergy sufferers schedule annual post-spring cleanings with us.

I've lived in my Carrollwood home for 25 years and never cleaned the ducts. What will you find?

In 25-year-uncleaned systems in Carrollwood, we typically find multiple layers of compacted debris including dust, pollen, pet hair, and often biological growth (mold or mildew). The debris load is usually substantial enough that we take before photos to document it. The transformation after cleaning is dramatic and homeowners consistently report improved air quality and better AC performance.

Do you service the Carrollwood Village and Carrollwood Country Club areas?

Yes. We service all Carrollwood-area neighborhoods including Carrollwood Village, original Carrollwood, and the surrounding Northdale and Lake Magdalene communities. Same flat-rate pricing throughout the area — no zone surcharges.
