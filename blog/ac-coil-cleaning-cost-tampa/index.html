AC Coil Cleaning Cost in Tampa, FL — Honest 2026 Pricing

One of the most common questions we get from Tampa homeowners is: how much does it actually cost to clean an AC coil? The short answer is that it ranges from $75 to over $400 depending on who you call — and that spread says more about the industry than about your HVAC system. This post breaks down what's included in a standard service, why prices vary so much, and exactly what we charge.

What a Standard AC Coil Cleaning Includes

A professional AC coil cleaning should always cover more than just the coil itself. The evaporator coil, blower wheel, and drain pan all work together inside your air handler, and cleaning one while ignoring the others leaves the job half-done.

Here's what should be included in every residential coil clean:

  • Evaporator coil cleaning — the coil sits inside the air handler and absorbs heat from indoor air. In Tampa's humidity, it accumulates a mixture of dust, biological growth, and moisture that reduces its ability to transfer heat efficiently.
  • Blower wheel cleaning — the squirrel-cage blower wheel that pushes air across the coil collects thick layers of debris on its fins. A dirty blower wheel reduces airflow and can throw the fan out of balance, causing noise and premature wear on the motor.
  • Drain pan cleaning and flush — the condensate drain pan sits below the coil and catches moisture. In Florida's climate, algae and slime grow in the pan constantly. A clogged pan overflows and causes water damage; a clean pan lets the system drain freely.

What Our $99 AC Coil Cleaning Covers

Our flat-rate $99 for a standard residential AC coil service includes all three of the above: evaporator coil, blower wheel, and drain pan — for one air handler unit. We use coil-safe foaming cleaners, flush the drain line, and inspect the system before and after. There are no add-on fees for the drain pan or blower wheel. What you're quoted is what you pay.

Why AC Coil Cleaning Prices Vary So Much in Tampa

We regularly hear from homeowners who've been quoted anywhere from $75 to $400+ for the same service. Here's what drives that range:

  • Scope of work — some companies quote "coil cleaning" but only spray the coil with cleaner and leave. Others do a full clean including blower wheel and drain pan. These are very different services at similar advertised prices.
  • Business model — large HVAC companies with high overhead and commission-based technicians charge more. Specialized cleaning companies with lower overhead can offer the same or better service for less.
  • Upsell structure — some companies quote low and then charge separately for the blower wheel ($50), drain pan ($40), and drain line flush ($30), arriving at $200+ for what should be one package.
  • Geographic demand — in summer, Tampa's HVAC service demand peaks. Emergency-rate pricing from HVAC contractors (who do coil cleaning as a side service) inflates costs significantly in June through September.

AC Drain Line Cleaning Cost — Is It Separate?

The condensate drain line runs from the drain pan to your home's exterior or a floor drain. In Florida's heat and humidity, algae and slime grow in this line rapidly — a blockage causes the pan to overflow and water to back up into your ceiling or walls. We've seen thousands of dollars in drywall damage from a clogged drain line that cost nothing to prevent.

Many companies charge $30–$60 as a separate add-on for flushing the drain line. We include drain line flushing as part of every standard AC coil cleaning service at no extra charge. When you book a coil clean with us, the drain line is part of the job.

When Does AC Coil Cleaning Cost More?

There are legitimate reasons a coil cleaning may be priced above our standard rate:

  • Two-system homes — if your home has two air handlers (common in larger Tampa homes over 2,500 sq ft), each unit is priced separately at $99 each
  • Packaged units — rooftop or closet-mounted packaged HVAC systems require different access and may take longer
  • Commercial or multi-family units — commercial air handlers are typically larger and require more labor and product
  • Condenser coil add-on — the outdoor condenser coil can be cleaned separately; ask for a quote when scheduling
  • Severely neglected systems — coils that haven't been cleaned in 5+ years with heavy biofilm may require additional rounds of treatment

AC Coil Cleaning vs Air Duct Cleaning — Which Do You Need First?

This is one of the most common questions we get. The honest answer is: it depends on your symptoms.

Start with AC coil cleaning if: your home isn't cooling efficiently, your electric bill has spiked, your system runs constantly, or you notice a musty smell immediately when the AC turns on (this often comes from the coil or drain pan, not the ducts).

Start with air duct cleaning if: you see visible dust coming from registers, rooms have uneven temperatures, you've recently renovated, or it's been 3+ years since your last cleaning.

If you're not sure, our combo package ($149) includes both services and is the most efficient way to address the full system. Most Tampa homeowners who haven't had either done in over two years benefit from both.

The ROI of AC Coil Cleaning in Florida

A dirty evaporator coil doesn't just make your home feel less comfortable — it costs you money every month. Research on HVAC efficiency consistently shows that a coil coated with biofilm and dust loses 15–25% of its heat transfer capacity. In Florida, where AC runs nearly year-round, that translates directly into higher electric bills.

The average Tampa household spends $150–$200 per month on electricity in summer, with 40–60% of that going to the AC system. A 20% efficiency loss on your AC means roughly $15–$25 extra per month — or $180–$300 per year. At $99 for a cleaning, the service pays for itself within a few months and improves your comfort immediately. That's why we recommend annual or biannual coil cleaning for Florida residents, not every five years as often stated on general HVAC guides written for northern climates.
