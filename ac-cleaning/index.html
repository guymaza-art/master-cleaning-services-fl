What's Included in $99 AC Cleaning

Our flat-rate residential AC coil cleaning covers the three components that cause 90% of Florida AC complaints.

1. Evaporator Coil Clean

The cold coil inside your air handler. Mold, dust, and biofilm coat the fins over time, reducing heat transfer and creating the musty smell. We use coil-safe cleaner and rinse to factory finish.

2. Blower Wheel Clean

The squirrel-cage fan that pushes air through the system. Dust cakes onto every fin and dramatically reduces airflow. Pull, scrub, and reinstall — most Tampa systems regain noticeable airflow immediately.

3. Drain Pan Flush

Standing water in the drain pan is mold's best friend. We flush the pan, clear the condensate line, and check for cracks or rust that signal the pan is past its lifespan.

Florida AC fact: Indoor humidity above 60% combined with a dust-coated evaporator coil = mold within weeks. Tampa homes with no maintenance for 3+ years almost always have visible biofilm on the coil.

AC Coil Cleaning FAQs — Tampa, FL

Why does my AC smell musty in Tampa?

The "dirty sock" smell is caused by mold and bacteria growing on a damp evaporator coil and inside the drain pan. In Tampa's humidity, condensate forms on the coil with every cooling cycle — that moisture plus organic dust creates ideal microbial habitat. Cleaning the coil, blower wheel, and drain pan eliminates the smell at its source. Air freshener won't fix it.

How much does AC coil cleaning cost in Tampa?

Our service starts at $99 and covers evaporator coil, blower wheel, and drain pan. Outdoor condenser coil cleaning is an add-on; EPA-registered antimicrobial sanitizer is optional. Firm quote after free inspection.

How often should I clean my AC coil in Florida?

For most Tampa Bay homes, every 1–2 years. Homes with pets, allergy sufferers, or heavy AC use benefit from annual cleaning. Indoor coils get dirty faster in Florida than in cooler climates because the AC runs more hours per year.

Will cleaning the AC coil lower my electric bill?

Usually yes, if the coil was visibly dirty. A coated evaporator coil restricts airflow and reduces heat-transfer efficiency, forcing longer run times. Tampa homes with heavy buildup can see 10–25% lower cooling costs after cleaning.

Do you also clean the outdoor condenser coil?

Yes — as an add-on. The outdoor unit pulls in pollen, grass clippings, lint, and dust which insulates the coil and forces the compressor to work harder. We rinse and brush the fins and clear debris from the cabinet.

Is AC coil cleaning the same as an AC tune-up?

No. A tune-up by an HVAC technician covers refrigerant, electrical connections, and motor health. We focus on physical cleaning of the coil, blower wheel, drain pan, and ductwork. The two services complement each other.

Do you replace the air filter?

Yes — we'll install a new MERV-rated filter at the customer's request and at cost. Filter replacement isn't part of the base $99 service because filter sizes vary, but it's the easiest add-on we offer.
